Money, credit cards and expenses

Understanding Money in Madagascar:

  • Currency: The Malagasy currency is the Ariary (MGA), divided into 5 Iraimbilanja.
  • Availability: Ariary is primarily available at local banks, official exchange offices in hotels, and the airport in Antananarivo.
  • Recommendation: We suggest bringing cash in Euros (EUR), British Pounds (GBP), or US Dollars (USD) for exchange. Tip: Exchanging a small amount (200-350 EUR) for Ariary upon arrival at the airport is convenient. You can exchange more throughout your trip as needed. Remember, leftover Ariary cannot be converted back to foreign currency, so exchange smaller amounts at a time. Important: Avoid black markets for currency exchange.

Credit Cards:

  • Limited Use: Credit cards are only accepted in some hotels, primarily in major cities like the capital (Antananarivo) and provincial capitals.
  • Fees Apply: Using your credit card may incur fees depending on your bank.
  • ATMs: Withdrawing cash from ATMs is possible at major banks, but ATMs are not widely available, especially in rural areas.

Bottom Line: Cash is king in Madagascar, especially outside major cities. Plan to use cash for most purchases.

Visa Requirements

For stays in Madagascar, a short-term visa is required. Here's a breakdown of the cost:

  • Less than 15 days: €10 (administrative fee)
  • More than 15 days: €35 (visa fee)

Obtaining Your Visa:

There are two convenient ways to secure your visa:

  1. Apply Online: This is the recommended option to avoid any potential delays upon arrival. Visit the official government website:  https://evisamada-mg.com/en/home
  1. Visa on Arrival: Obtain your visa upon arrival at the airport immigration office by presenting your passport.

Tips and gratuities

We believe tipping is a personal choice for each traveler. However, it's important to understand that tipping is customary in Madagascar, where wages tend to be lower. Consider tipping to show your appreciation for good service.

Here's a general guideline:

  • Drivers: €5 USD per day
  • Local Guides: €3-€5 USD per day
  • National Guides: $8-$10 USD per day
  • Restaurant Waiters: 10% of the bill (adjust based on service quality)

Explanation:

  • Drivers: Drivers typically receive a smaller tip compared to guides.
  • Local Guides: Local guides, who might be specific to a town or region, can be tipped in this range.
  • National Guides: National guides, who are often more experienced and have broader knowledge, can receive a slightly higher tip.

Itinerary

Overnight : Amazon Hotel ★★★

Day 3 : Oct 4 ⇒ Drive from Toliara to Ranohira

Depart on a 4x4 journey towards Ranohira, passing through captivating desert landscapes and charming villages. After approximately 2 hours, explore the unique biodiversity oasis of Zombitse-Vohibasia National Park, hiking through the lush rainforest to encounter lemurs and vibrant flora. Continue to Ranohira, arriving in the late afternoon.

Overnight : Jardin du Roy ★★★★

Day 4 : Oct 5 => Isalo (Visit National parc + Trekking to the Natural swimming pool)

Today, you'll explore the breathtaking Isalo National Park, a true gem of Madagascar's natural wonders. Embark on a guided hike through the park's rugged landscapes, marveling at the massive sandstone formations that have been sculpted by wind and water over millions of years. Keep your eyes peeled for the park's diverse flora and fauna, including the iconic ring-tailed lemurs and the elusive Barter's antelope.

One of the highlights of your visit will be the trek to the natural swimming pool, a hidden oasis amidst the arid surroundings. Follow your guide along the winding trails, taking in the stunning vistas of the Isalo Massif, before arriving at this serene spot. Cool off in the refreshing waters of the natural pool, surrounded by towering rock formations that create a striking contrast against the crystal-clear waters.

After an invigorating day of exploring, you'll have the opportunity to witness a spectacular sunset over the Isalo landscapes, painting the rocky formations in warm hues of orange and red – a truly unforgettable sight.

Overnight : Le Jardin du Roy ★★★★

Day 5 : Oct 6 => Isalo - Ambalavao (Anja parc)

Depart from Ranohira early in the morning, leaving behind the Isalo's mesmerizing sandstone formations.

Embark on a 4x4 adventure, traversing the vast landscapes of southern Madagascar, passing through arid plateaus and encountering traditional villages.

Arrive at the Anja Community Reserve, a haven for lemurs and other remarkable wildlife. Explore the spiny forest, a unique ecosystem with towering succulents and fascinating creatures.

Note on Anja Community Reserve: All animals in the pac are in the wild but not in captivity.

Overnight : Betsileo Country Lodge

Day 6 : Oct 7 ⇒ Ambalavao - Ranomafana (Night walk visit in the NP)

Continue the journey towards Ranomafana, witnessing the contrasting beauty of lush valleys and cascading waterfalls.

Night walk visit in Ranomafana National Parc

Immerse yourself in the fascinating world of the Ranomafana rainforest on an unforgettable nocturnal tour. Explore the mysterious moonlit trails and observe a variety of nocturnal species in their natural habitat.

Meet bright-eyed nocturnal lemurs such as the red-bellied lemur, Milne-Edwards' grey lemur and dwarf lemur, moving gracefully through the tree branches. Admire the shimmering colors of nocturnal chameleons camouflaged in the lush vegetation. Be enchanted by the songs of the frogs and discover the discreet snakes that criss-cross the forest floor.

Further information:

  • Duration: approx. 2-3 hours
  • Difficulty: easy to moderate
  • Season: all year round

Tips:

  • Bring a flashlight or headlamp.
  • Wear comfortable clothing suitable for walking.
  • Apply insect repellent.
  • Respect the flora and fauna.

Overnight : Thermal Hotel ★★★

Day 7 : Oct 8 => Ranomafana (National parc visit + Swimming in the thermal pool )

Start your adventure by immersing yourself in the verdant expanses of Ranomafana National Park, where a guided hike unveils a world brimming with unique wildlife, including several species of lemur and exotic birds, all of which thrive in this dynamic ecosystem. Immerse yourself in the natural beauty and biodiversity that make this park one of Madagascar's jewels.

After your exploration, move on to a state of relaxation at Ranomafana's thermal pool. Here, mineral-infused hot waters offer a therapeutic escape, allowing you to relax and rejuvenate against the serene backdrop of the park's forested landscape. This combination of adventure and relaxation is an enriching, well-balanced experience in one of Madagascar's most popular natural settings.

Overnight : Thermal Hotel ★★★

Day 8 : Oct 9 => Ranomafana - Antsirabe (Road Trip)

Early departure from Ranomafana, leaving behind the lush beauty of the rainforest.

Stop at the Namorona waterfall, a spectacular waterfall nestled in a green setting. Admire the power of the rushing water and capture unforgettable photos of this awe-inspiring landscape.

Continue on to Ambositra, a town renowned for its wood crafts.

Immerse yourself in the artistic world of Ambositra by visiting the woodcarving art galleries. Admire the talent of local craftsmen who transform wood into veritable works of art. Discover the different carving techniques and be charmed by the finesse and precision of the sculptures.

Depart Ambositra and continue to Antsirabe.
Arrival in Antsirabe in the late afternoon. Check into your hotel and discover the city, nicknamed "the city of waters". Enjoy the peaceful, relaxing atmosphere and let yourself be charmed by the many lakes and hot springs.

Tips:

  • Don't forget your cameras to capture the scenery and sculptures.
  • Bring spare change to buy souvenirs in the art galleries.
  • Bring warm clothes for the evening in Antsirabe.

Overnight : Royal Palace Hotel ★★★

Day 9 - Oct 10 : Drive from Antsirabe to Miandrivazo (City Tour + Tritriva & Andraikiba lake visit)

Early in the morning, explore the town of Antsirabe, then continue on to Lake Tritriva, a magnificent place full of history, then visit Lake Andraikiba, after which continue on to Miandrivazo.

We depart to Miandrivazo located roughly 220 km west of Antsirabe. This is the starting point for boat trip along the Tsiribihina River. There will be stops en route contemplating nice viewpoint, encounter with the locals and other stops arranged by your guide.

Overnight : Soalia Hotel ★★★

This morning, we will drive to Masiakampy. When arriving at Masiakampy, board on a boat going to the waterfall. We shall have a window of opportunities along this river trip expecting to see not only the landscapes but also animals like birds, chameleons, various species of lemurs including the sifakas and the brown lemurs

After breakfast, back to the barge sailing down the Tsiribihina while passing several traditional villages. We shall stop by Berevo village in hope to learn about their daily life and cultures. When we get by the small port of Belo Sur Tsiribihina, we get off and then drive to Belo Tsiribihina, the cultural capital of the Sakalava tribe.

Day 12 - Oct 13 : Belo-sur-Tsiribihina - Bekopaka 🚘

This morning, we drive to Bekopaka with stops en route. Bekopaka is the village just by the national park of Bemaraha where the picturesque view of the limestone pinnacles will surprise visitors because of its natural beauty and the exceptional biodiversity it possesses.

Overnight: Solei des Tsingy ★★★

Day 13 - Oct 14 ⇒ Bekopaka (Small Tsingy visit + Gorge of Manambolo river)

Today you'll discover the Small Tsingy, a maze-like network of sharp limestone needles. Navigate through this intricate geological wonder, marveling at nature's artistry.

Later, venture to the awe-inspiring Gorge of Manambolo River, a deep ravine carved by powerful waters over centuries. Take in the breathtaking views of the gorge's towering walls adorned with lush vegetation and cascading waterfalls. Keep an eye out for the park's unique flora and fauna, including endemic lemurs, birds, and reptiles.

Overnight : Soleil Des Tsingy Lodge ★★★

Day 14 - Oct 15 ⇒ Bekopaka (Big Tsingy visit - Climbing + Hiking + Suspension bridge)

Today, you'll embark on an extraordinary adventure to the Grand Tsingy, a geological wonder within the Tsingy de Bemaraha National Park. This UNESCO World Heritage Site boasts a unique and surreal landscape of towering limestone pinnacles, carved by natural erosion over millions of years. After a briefing from your expert guide, you'll set off on a thrilling hike through this otherworldly terrain, navigating narrow passages and scaling ladders to explore the intricate network of canyons and caves. Along the way, you'll have the opportunity to spot the park's diverse flora and fauna, including several endemic species found nowhere else on Earth. Towards the end of your exploration, you'll cross the iconic suspension bridge, suspended high above the canyon, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding tsingy formations. The Grand Tsingy promises to be a highlight of your Madagascan adventure, offering an unforgettable experience amidst one of nature's most remarkable creations.

Overnight : Soleil Des Tsingy ★★★

Day 15 : Oct 16 => Bekopaka - Sunset at Baobab alley - Morondava

Depart Bekopaka, embarking on the scenic drive back to Morondava. Evening highlights include witnessing the iconic Baobab Alley at sunset - a mesmerizing sight as the golden rays bathe the ancient baobab trees. Capture unforgettable photographs of these majestic giants silhouetted against the vibrant sky. After this breathtaking spectacle, continue to Morondava for an overnight stay, reflecting on the day's extraordinary experiences.
